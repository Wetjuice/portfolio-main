01

Ashen Meridian

2019
Open-World RPG

A dying sun-empire where every NPC lives a full simulated life. 12M copies sold.

Set in a collapsing civilization powered by a fading star, Ashen Meridian redefined emergent storytelling. Every character — from emperors to street vendors — operates on a full life simulation, creating narratives no designer could have scripted. A landmark for the medium.

Game of the Year — TGA 2019 Best Direction — BAFTA 2020 Best Narrative — GDC 2020
02

HOLLOW

2014
Psychological Horror

Non-linear dread mechanics and an unreliable narrator. 2M copies. Cult classic.

HOLLOW weaponized uncertainty. The game's narrator lies. The environment shifts when you're not looking. Critics called it "the Inland Empire of games" — a work that trusts its audience enough to leave them unsettled and questioning what they actually experienced.

Cult Classic Award — IGF 2015
03

BREACH: Overwatch Protocol

2022
Tactical Multiplayer

Asymmetric class design with a persistent world narrative. 8M active players.

BREACH proved that competitive multiplayer and deep narrative aren't mutually exclusive. Its world evolves between seasons through player actions, and its asymmetric class system made every match feel like a tactical puzzle with no single correct answer.

Best Multiplayer — D.I.C.E. 2023
