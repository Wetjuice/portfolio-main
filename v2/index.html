How I Think

Three things I've
stopped arguing about.

01
Systems Over Stories

"The most memorable gaming moments were never scripted — they happened because the systems were good enough to generate something nobody planned."

Linear narrative is a solved problem. Every other medium does it better. What games uniquely produce — the emergent ambush, the spontaneous alliance, the story no writer wrote — only happens when the underlying systems are strong enough to surprise even their authors. That's the target.

02
Test Ugly. Ship Clean.

"Fidelity is a reward for ideas that survive contact with reality. If you're polishing before validating, you're spending craft on faith."

A graybox prototype that teaches you the core loop is working isn't broken — it's the most efficient design tool you have. The discipline is holding the line on polish until the idea has earned it. Most studios fail here not for lack of taste, but lack of process confidence.

03
Direction Is Recognition, Not Invention

"The most valuable thing a director does isn't generate the answer — it's recognise it when the team brings it in, and commit."

Leaders who insist on originating every solution bottleneck the team and signal distrust. Build teams smart enough to generate options you wouldn't have thought of yourself. Then make the call — clearly, quickly, and with enough conviction that the team can execute without second-guessing.
