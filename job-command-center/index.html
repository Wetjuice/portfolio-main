🔑 key market insights
Live-Service Collapse Creates Openings
11 of 19 major live-service launches in 2025 lost 80–99% of players. Studios are pivoting back to single-player or hybrid models — your Bend experience (live-service cancellation → new project) is a narrative asset, not a liability.
Director Supply Exceeds Demand (For Now)
Mass layoffs flooded the senior market in 2024–2025. But: studios are rebuilding with leaner teams and need proven leaders who've survived restructuring. Your "stayed through 30% cuts and got promoted" story is rare and valuable.
Gaming → Tech Pipeline Is Hot
Netflix, Amazon, Apple, Google all building games divisions. They're hungry for gaming-native product leaders who speak both game design and product management. Your Product Owner → Game Director arc bridges both worlds.
Remote Director Roles Are Rare But Growing
Most director roles still require hybrid/on-site. Netflix Games and Xsolla are exceptions. For remote flexibility with a baby incoming, tech-product crossover roles offer more options than pure game studio positions.

🎯 positioning strategy
Your Two-Track Approach
Track 1: Gaming Director — You're a Game Director with 16 years and shipped titles. Lead with authority, not apology. The Bend restructuring story is an asset: "I stayed through 30% cuts, got promoted to Game Director, and am now building the next project."

Track 2: Tech/Product — Your Product Owner → Game Director arc translates directly to Product Director. The gaming-to-tech pipeline is hot. Lead with the metrics: "50% timeline compression, 90-person team, $0→live revenue system." Don't explain why you're leaving games — explain what you bring from games.
