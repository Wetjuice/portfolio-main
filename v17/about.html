About

The best game design is invisible.

It doesn't announce itself. It doesn't explain what you should feel or what you should do next. It earns trust by making the right choice obvious — and then gets out of the way.

I believe players are smarter than we give them credit for. That progression systems shouldn't be treadmills. That the graybox phase is where the real design happens — not in the pitch deck. Over 16 years and five studios, I've directed, designed, and shipped games that put systems integrity above spectacle. As Game Director at Sony Bend Studio, I lead creative vision and multidisciplinary teams on an unannounced title. Before that, I held design leadership roles at Deep Silver Volition on Saints Row, Pocket Gems on War Dragons, Tencent, and Petroglyph.

Design Thinking

What I believe.

01 Systems Punch Above Their Weight +

The best cost-to-experience ratio isn't bigger budgets — it's systems that create more than they cost.

When you design interconnected rules instead of isolated features, you get emergent behavior — moments the team never scripted but players will never forget. Systems that talk to each other compound value exponentially.

02 Validate Before You Polish +

Fidelity is a reward for ideas that survive contact with reality. Find the fun first.

Test fast, test ugly. The prettiest prototype in the world is worthless if the core loop doesn't hold up. Graybox ruthlessly, validate with real players, and only then invest in polish.

03 Ideas Are Abundant. Execution Is Scarce. +

Design isn't about generating good ideas. Everyone has good ideas. Great design is identifying which ones to pursue — then making them real.

The hardest part of direction isn't ideation — it's selection and follow-through. Saying no to good ideas so you can say yes to the right ones is what separates shipped games from eternal prototypes.

04 Leadership Is Selection, Not Invention +

The most valuable thing a director does isn't generate the answer — it's recognise it when the team brings it in, and commit to it.

Great teams produce great ideas constantly. The director's job is to cultivate an environment where those ideas surface, recognize the ones that serve the vision, and have the conviction to commit.

05 Emergence Is the Medium's Superpower +

The most memorable gaming moments were never scripted.

Film can craft a perfect scene. Music can build a perfect crescendo. Only games can create moments that surprise even their creators. Design for emergence and you design for infinite replayability.

06 Sustainable Systems Over Content Treadmills +

A game that needs constant content injection to feel alive has a design problem underneath.

Content is expensive. Systems are scalable. Build mechanics that generate variety through player agency and systemic interaction, and you reduce your dependency on the content treadmill.

07 A Game for Everyone Is a Game for No One +

Every decision made to broaden appeal dilutes conviction.

The games that endure are the ones that know exactly who they're for and serve that audience with unwavering commitment. Trying to please everyone is the fastest path to mediocrity.

08 Clarity Is Kindness +

Predictable rules. Unpredictable outcomes.

Players should always understand the system. They should never be able to fully predict what happens next. Clarity in rules creates trust; surprise in outcomes creates delight.
