01 philosophy
"The best games don't guide you — they give you tools and let you write your own story."

systems over scripts

I don't believe in hand-holding. Players are smarter than we give them credit for. Build interconnected systems, establish clear rules, and step back. The stories that emerge from player choice are always more meaningful than anything we could write.

respect the player

Every design decision should ask: does this empower the player or patronize them? Challenge is respect. Ambiguity is respect. Letting them fail — and learn — is respect.

obsess over feel

A game's soul lives in the first 30 seconds. The weight of a jump. The snap of a menu. The rhythm of combat. If it doesn't feel right, nothing else matters.
