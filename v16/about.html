About

The best game design is invisible.

It doesn't announce itself. It doesn't explain what you should feel or what you should do next. It earns trust by making the right choice obvious — and then gets out of the way.

Players are smarter than we give them credit for. Progression systems shouldn't be treadmills. The graybox phase — when the game is ugly and honest — is where the real design happens. These aren't theories. They're conclusions drawn from 16 years of building games that either proved or disproved them.

I'm Michael Berman. I'm a Game Director at Sony Bend, where I'm currently leading an unannounced title. Before that: Deep Silver Volition (Saints Row), Pocket Gems (War Dragons), Tencent, and Petroglyph.

Design Thinking

What I believe.

01 Systems Punch Above Their Weight +

The best cost-to-experience ratio isn't bigger budgets — it's systems that create more than they cost.

When interconnected rules produce emergent behavior, you get hundreds of hours of player engagement from a fraction of the content investment.

02 Validate Before You Polish +

Fidelity is a reward for ideas that survive contact with reality. Find the fun first.

The moment you polish before validating, you're spending craft on an unproven idea. Test fast, test ugly, iterate honestly.

03 Ideas Are Abundant. Execution Is Scarce. +

Design isn't about generating good ideas. Everyone has good ideas. Great design is identifying which ones to pursue — then making them real.

The gap between a promising concept and a shipped feature is where real design work lives.

04 Leadership Is Selection, Not Invention +

The most valuable thing a director does isn't generate the answer — it's recognise it when the team brings it in, and commit to it.

Build teams smart enough to generate options you wouldn't have thought of yourself. Then make the call.

05 Emergence Is the Medium's Superpower +

The most memorable gaming moments were never scripted. They happened because the systems were good enough to generate something nobody planned.

The unplanned ambush, the spontaneous alliance, the emergent story no writer wrote — those happen when systems and social dynamics collide unexpectedly.

06 Sustainable Systems Over Content Treadmills +

A game that needs constant content injection to feel alive has a design problem underneath.

Build systems that generate interesting decisions. Let content amplify that depth. Never let it substitute.

07 A Game for Everyone Is a Game for No One +

Every decision made to broaden appeal dilutes conviction.

Define your audience specifically. Design for that person with full commitment.

08 Clarity Is Kindness +

Predictable rules. Unpredictable outcomes. If players can forecast what their actions produce, they can make meaningful choices.

Consistent mechanics build mental models. Mental models build player agency. Agency creates ownership of outcomes.
